# TeamTreasure Brand Voice & Terminology Guide

## Introduction

### Core Principle: Protection, Not Policing

TeamTreasure serves volunteer treasurers in tight-knit hockey communities where parents know each other personally. While our platform provides robust fraud prevention capabilities (essential post-OMHA scandal), our **internal UI language must protect and support treasurers**, not make them feel suspected or surveilled.

**The Rule:**

## Tone Guidelines

### In-App Tone: Supportive & Protective

**Principles:**
- **You're on their team:** "We know you're doing a great job"
- **Protect them:** "This keeps you safe from accusations"
- **Collaborative:** "Team approach to finances"
- **Professional but warm:** Friendly without being casual

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Second person ("you", "your")
- Active voice, short sentences
- Focus on benefits to the treasurer
- Acknowledge their volunteer role
- Emphasize trust-building with parents

**Example Tone:**
> "Two-person approval protects you from questions at year-end. Parents see that every expense has independent verification, which builds confidence in your financial management."

### Marketing Tone: Confident & Strategic

**Principles:**
- **Direct about fraud:** "Prevents embezzlement"
- **Reference industry context:** "Post-OMHA scandal"
- **ROI-focused:** "Saves leagues from financial losses"
- **Authority positioning:** "Industry-leading security"

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Third person for credibility
- Strong action verbs
- Reference specific threats
- Emphasize competitive advantage
- Compliance and legal protection angle

**Example Tone:**
> "TeamTreasure's fraud prevention system catches financial irregularities before they become embezzlement scandals. After the $2.4M OMHA incident, leagues need robust safeguards."

### League/B2B Tone: Serious & Compliance-Focused

**Principles:**
- **Risk mitigation:** Emphasize what could go wrong
- **Regulatory compliance:** League rules, tax requirements
- **Fiduciary responsibility:** Legal obligations
- **Professional standards:** Industry best practices

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Formal but not stuffy
- Focus on liability protection
- Reference regulations and standards
- Use industry terminology appropriately
- Emphasize institutional benefits

**Example Tone:**
> "League administrators have a fiduciary responsibility to protect member funds. TeamTreasure ensures compliance with financial oversight requirements while maintaining complete audit trails for year-end reporting."

## Special Cases

### 1. Legal/Required Language

Sometimes you MUST use specific terminology for legal compliance (e.g., "Audit Trail" in API documentation for accountants). In these cases:

- Use the required term in backend/technical contexts
- Use friendlier synonyms in user-facing UI
- Example: Database field = `audit_trail`, UI label = "Transaction History"

### 2. Parent/Public-Facing Features

If parents see the UI (like a public transaction view):

- Use **even more** trust-building language
- Focus on transparency and verification
- Example: "Every expense verified by two team members" (not "dual authorization")

### 3. League Admin Dashboards

For league-level users managing multiple teams:

- More serious tone is acceptable
- Can reference compliance requirements
- Still avoid overtly accusatory language
- Example: "Ensure all teams meet oversight requirements" (not "Monitor teams for fraud")

### 4. Error States (Technical Issues)

Even technical errors should maintain supportive tone:

âŒ **BAD:** "Transaction rejected: fraud detection system failure"
âœ… **GOOD:** "We couldn't process this transaction. Please try again or contact support."
